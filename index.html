Marley performing in 1980.

    Here's a timeline of Robert Nesta Marley's life

  • 1945- Born in Nine Mile, Saint Ann Parish, Jamaica
  • 1962- Marley recorded four songs, "Judge Not", "One Cup of Coffee", "Do You Still Love Me?" and "Terror"
  • 1963- Bob Marley, Bunny Wailer, Peter Tosh, Junior Braithwaite, Beverley Kelso, and Cherry Smith were called the Teenagers.They later changed the name to the Wailing Rudeboys, then to the Wailing Wailers, at which point they were discovered by record producer Coxsone Dodd, and finally to the Wailers.
  • 1966- Marley married Rita Anderson
  • 1968- He was arrested after being caught with cannabis, but continued to use marijuana in accordance with his religious beliefs
  • 1972- Bob Marley signed with CBS Records in London and embarked on a UK tour with American soul singer Johnny Nash.
  • 1977- Marley was found to have a type of malignant melanoma under the nail of a toe.
  • 1979- Tracks such as "Zimbabwe", "Africa Unite", "Wake Up and Live", and "Survival" reflected Marley's support for the struggles of Africans.
  • 1980- Bob was invited to perform at 17 April celebration of Zimbabwe's Independence Day.
  • 1980- Bob Marley's final studio album, and is one of his most religious productions; it includes "Redemption Song" and "Forever Loving Jah".
  • 1981- dies at the age of 36.
